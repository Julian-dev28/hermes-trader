### HOT LEAD — start here (already measured, confirm and act)

A stop-width sweep on `scripts/backtest.py` over the **same** 21-day / 30-coin
trade population (live exit geometry: protect 1.0, retrace 0.40) shows the live
**`dsl_exit.max_loss_pct = 1.2%` is the primary PnL leak** — it sits *inside the
noise band* of these coins (4h ATR routinely 2–5%), so price stops us out and
then recovers without us:

| `max_loss_pct` | win rate | expectancy/trade | total PnL (21d, $100) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1.2% (LIVE)** | 48.6% | **−$0.003** | **−16%** ❌ |
| 1.8% | 59.5% | +$0.033 | +157% |
| 2.5% | 68.0% | +$0.094 | +393% |
| 3.5% | 77.0% | +$0.226 | +782% |

Monotonic: every widening of the stop raises expectancy. The 1.2% was set during
a round-trip-give-back panic and overcorrected a +EV system into −EV. **The right
fix is NOT an ultra-tight entry stop** — it's a sane-width volatility-scaled stop
(let the trade breathe) PLUS the breakeven ratchet + TP scale-out to handle
give-back. Reproduce this, find the PnL-optimal width (consider an **ATR-multiple
stop** instead of a fixed %), confirm it survives `backtest_logged.py` /
`backtest_full.py` on the real AI verdicts, and treat it as proposal #1. Mind the
`max_loss_roe_pct` leverage cap interaction in `dsl_exit.py`.
